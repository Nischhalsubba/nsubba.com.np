Fintech Mobile App

Nova Financial

Redefining the mobile banking experience for a new generation of investors.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

3 Months

Client

Nova Corp

The Challenge My Approach Process & Design The Outcome

The Challenge

Nova Financial wanted to expand their user base to include Gen Z investors. However, their legacy application was cluttered, slow, and full of financial jargon that intimidated new users. The drop-off rate during onboarding was nearly 60%.

Goal: Simplify the interface without losing advanced functionality required by power users, and increase retention by 20%.

My Approach

  • Discovery: Conducted user interviews with 15 participants aged 18-25 to understand investing habits and jargon pain-points.
  • Audit: Analyzed key competitors (Robinhood, Revolut, Monzo) to identify UX gaps and opportunity areas.
  • Strategy: Proposed a "Progressive Disclosure" model where complex tools are hidden until needed.

Process & Design

I started with low-fidelity wireframes focusing on a "One action per screen" philosophy for the onboarding flow. This reduced cognitive load significantly.

For the UI, I developed a dark-mode first system to align with modern aesthetic preferences and battery saving needs. We used vibrant green accents to signal positive financial growth, but kept the rest of the palette muted.

High-fidelity mockups showing the progressive onboarding flow.

We also introduced "Nova Academy", a gamified learning hub that rewards users for learning financial literacy. This required close collaboration with the content team to ensure the design supported bite-sized learning.

The Outcome

After a 3-month redesign and development cycle, we launched the V2 app to a test group of 10,000 users.

  • Retention: Day-30 retention increased by 15%, missing the 20% goal but still a massive improvement.
  • Onboarding: Completion rate jumped from 40% to 75%.
  • Feedback: Users specifically cited the "clean look" and "lack of confusion" in App Store reviews.

This project taught me the importance of stripping away features to add value. Sometimes, less really is more.
